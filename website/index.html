My Journey to Web Development

I left my teaching post in the summer of 2022 with the initial intention of making a career move into EdTech. During my final year, my school was visited by an EdTech specialist and I took on a new role as Digital Leader. It was my responsibility to trial new uses of EdTech in the classroom and help develop a school-wide approach to digital learning. This experience strengthened my belief in the potential benefits of technology to education and inspired me to seek work in the EdTech space. As I looked at jobs at EdTech companies, I was struck by the idea that the truly exciting aspects of their products, 'where the magic happens', were coming from the tech side of their business. While the content that they sought to deliver was more or less the same, the real power behind the best platforms was the functionality of their apps.

I had been interested in learning to code for a long time. My own experiences at an EdTech start-up, those from friends and family who work in tech, the prominence of technology in my day-to-day life and my love of problem-solving, linguistics, design and (as I discovered to my surprise when teaching it!) maths were all factors in tempting me to make the transition, and my exploration of the EdTech space was the final push that I needed. I searched for coding bootcamps and came across Founders and Coders. I loved their collaborative, community-based approach to problem-solving and socially conscious ethos, as well as the fact that I could learn the full modern web-stack alongside getting paid as an apprentice.
